## 🔪 2. The Rejection Note (Killing a Darling)

**What I generated, loved, and ultimately rejected:**
I prompted an AI image generator to create a "futuristic, glowing neon server room" for the Background Jobs case study. It looked incredibly cool and cinematic. 

**Why I rejected it:**
I rejected it because it was pure decoration. It looked like stock art and didn't prove a single thing about my technical ability. Worse, its loud neon colors completely violated the clinical, understated mood established in my Identity Kit. I killed the darling and replaced it with the raw terminal screenshot because a terminal proves I actually wrote the Node.js queue logic.

## ⚖️ 3. Where I Chose Real Over AI

I strictly enforced a rule: **If it is the subject of the proof, it must be real.**

1. **The Case Studies:** I chose real screenshots over AI-generated isometric illustrations of "code". The engineering managers I am targeting (my "One Person") want to see real interfaces and real terminal outputs, not abstract AI art. 
2. **The Bio:** I chose a real photograph of myself over a slick AI-generated avatar. If the goal of the bio page is to build human trust right before they click the "Email Me" button, using a fake AI face shatters that trust immediately.
